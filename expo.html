Note on smoothness of solutions to heat equation

The purpose of this note is to give a very specific case for which we can easily show that the heat propogator $e^t\Delta$ makes initial data that is not C^\infty smooth for all subsequent time. This note is a rough sketch of an "appendix" to a final project for class on studying atmospheric heat convection using PDE models for geophysical flows (specifically Boussinesq type equations with Coriolis force). I claimed without proof that parabolic differential operators smooth out rough initial data and wanted to illustrate a specific case to at least "convince" readers. The statement proven is not at all general but the assumptions were made to make the argument easy and clear. In general, we can give bounds on W^s,p norms of e^t\Delta f for rough initial data which tell us more quantitatively the regularity and integrability of e^t\Delta f in terms of that of f. See Adams and Fournier. These notes may be updated later , both cleaned up and with expositions of these more general estimates. Email me if you're interested in this!
